What Companies Made the Top Five Billionaires?

    Amazon

  • Amazon revenue for the quarter ending June 30, 2021 was $113.080B, a 27.18% increase year-over-year.
  • Amazon revenue for the twelve months ending June 30, 2021 was $443.298B, a 37.76% increase year-over-year.
  • Amazon annual revenue for 2020 was $386.064B, a 37.62% increase from 2019.

    Tesla SpaceX

  • SpaceX completed an equity funding round of $850 million last week, people familiar with the financing told CNBC, sending the company’s valuation to about $74 billion.
  • The company raised the new funds at $419.99 a share, those people said, or just 1 cent below the $420 price that Elon Musk made infamous in 2018.

    Louis Vuitton

  • Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton (LVMH) is one of the world’s leading luxury groups, parent to 75 luxury houses. Over the past decade, LVMH enjoyed ever-increasing global revenue, reaching over 53 billion euros in 2019.
  • In 2020, reflecting the impact of the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic on luxury goods, the group’s revenue saw a reduction of around 17 percent, ultimately amounting to 44.6 billion euros for the year.

    Microsoft

  • Microsoft revenue for the quarter ending June 30, 2021 was $46.152B, a 21.35% increase year-over-year.
  • Microsoft revenue for the twelve months ending June 30, 2021 was $168.088B, a 17.53% increase year-over-year.

    Facebook

  • Facebook revenue for the quarter ending June 30, 2021 was $29.077B, a 55.6% increase year-over-year.
  • Facebook revenue for the twelve months ending June 30, 2021 was $104.789B, a 39.42% increase year-over-year.
